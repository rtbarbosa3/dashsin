Highlights · multi-year regime shift

SE/CO average PLD rose 69% from 2024 to 2025, then another 47% YTD into 2026. Trajectory: R$ 125.70 → R$ 212.63 → R$ 313.30. Sul is the most exposed: R$ 125.73 → R$ 216.27 → R$ 368.87 (+193% over 24 months).

The piso-floor regime that defined 2024 has dissolved. In 2024, all four submarkets averaged at the floor (R$ 61.07) for 5 of 12 months; in 2026 YTD, the floor has not been touched in any submarket. ENA/EAR trajectory has shifted the cost curve up structurally.

NE/N remain the cheapest submarkets across all three years — 2026 YTD averages of R$ 289.55 and R$ 290.46 vs SE/CO at R$ 313.30 and Sul at R$ 368.87. NE→SE/CO intercâmbio remains the binding constraint that prices this gap.
