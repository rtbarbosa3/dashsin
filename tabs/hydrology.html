Highlights · the EAR map explains the PLD map

Sul at 41.5% EAR is the operational risk concentration of the SIN. All three other subsystems sit at or above their long-term comfort zone (SE/CO 65.6%, NE 95.4%, N 96.6%), but Sul's 8,500 MWmês is −43 percentage points below the next-lowest. This is the direct hydrological driver behind Sul's PLD premium in March 2026 (avg R$ 462.51/MWh).

Inflow is reversing the picture for Sul. ENA bruta in Sul is running at 123% of long-term mean for the month-to-date — the highest of any subsystem. SE/CO is at 84%, NE only at 72%, N at 99%. If sustained, the wet-period recovery for Sul will widen the NE→SE/CO transmission stress: NE/N reservoirs already at cap means most incremental ENA has nowhere to be stored.

São Francisco basin at 95.5% storage — Sobradinho specifically at 98.83% useful volume. The basin alone supplies 96.7% of the NE subsystem's hydro storage, which makes the NE the most resilient subsystem in the SIN for the dry season ahead.
